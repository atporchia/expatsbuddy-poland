The **karta informacyjna leczenia szpitalnego** — often called the discharge
document or *wypis ze szpitala* — is the document a hospital gives you when a
hospital stay ends. In general terms it summarizes the stay: why you were
admitted, what was diagnosed and done, and recommendations for after
discharge. It is one of the documents most often requested afterwards, for
example by insurers or other doctors.

Poland keeps much of this documentation electronically. Official
pacjent.gov.pl pages describe the **electronic medical documentation (EDM)**
system and the **Internetowe Konto Pacjenta (IKP)** — the online patient
account where hospital discharge summaries, test results, and other records
can appear, including in the **mojeIKP** mobile app. If you have a PESEL
number, an IKP account already exists for you; you log in rather than
register.

Related concepts you may meet around a hospital stay:

- **skierowanie / e-skierowanie** — a referral to hospital or specialist
  treatment, issued electronically;
- **SOR** — the hospital emergency department, which official pages describe
  as being for **sudden threats to life or health**, not a replacement for a
  GP or specialist visit.

This page and this site do not read or interpret medical content. For what a
diagnosis or recommendation means, talk to a doctor; for urgent situations,
call **112** or go to SOR.
