A **JDG** (*jednoosobowa działalność gospodarcza*) is a one-person business —
the most common way foreigners and Polish citizens alike become
self-employed in Poland. Official sources describe it as simple to set up:
one person registers, runs the business under their own name, and is
personally liable for it (unlike a limited-liability company).

Registering a JDG is not a single, isolated step. Through the **CEIDG**
system, one application generally triggers several things at once: entry in
the business register, a **NIP** (tax number) from the tax office, a
**REGON** (statistical number) from the statistics office, and registration
with **ZUS** as the insurance payer. You also choose one or more **PKD**
codes describing what your business does.

A JDG is one of several legal forms for doing business in Poland. Official
sources contrast it with **spółka z o.o.** (a limited-liability company),
which is a separate legal entity registered through a different system
(**KRS**, not CEIDG) — generally more complex to set up and run, but with
liability limited to the company rather than the owner personally. Most
foreigners working independently in Poland, especially in **B2B**
arrangements, use a JDG rather than a spółka z o.o., but which form fits a
specific situation is a decision for the person involved, ideally with an
accountant.

For non-EU/EEA/Swiss/US citizens, official sources note an extra condition:
you generally need a residence title that allows you to run a business in
Poland. Official biznes.gov.pl pages describe which residence titles qualify
and what to expect at each step — that is the reference to check before
registering.

**Ukrainian citizens** specifically saw a bigger change: official sources
describe simplified wartime access to JDG registration as having ended on
**5 March 2026**, replaced by the same **PESEL UKR** or qualifying
residence-title requirement used for other non-EU citizens. Businesses
already registered by 4 March 2026 can continue operating while the
owner's stay in Poland remains legal, according to official sources.
