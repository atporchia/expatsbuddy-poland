"Residence based on family" is not a single procedure in Poland. The official
pages describe **several distinct paths**, and which one is relevant depends
mostly on who your family member is:

- **Family member of a Polish citizen** — for example, the spouse of a Polish
  citizen has a dedicated path, and official sources describe related paths
  for partners, stepchildren, and other relatives.
- **Family member of a non-EU foreigner living in Poland** — usually called
  **family reunification** (*połączenie z rodziną*), for example the non-EU
  spouse of a foreigner who already has a residence permit here.
- **Family member of an EU citizen** — a separate set of rules connected to EU
  free movement, different from both paths above.

Because the paths differ, the document lists differ too. Official sources
commonly mention civil-status documents such as **marriage certificates** and
**birth certificates**, and for documents issued abroad they often mention
**legalization or an apostille**. The exact list for your path is on the
official MOS page for that path.

Applications go through the MOS portal and are decided by the voivode. If
formal elements are missing, the office sends a request for filling formal
gaps (*braki formalne*) with a deadline it sets. Start from the official
family-paths overview linked below and pick the page matching your family
situation — and when in doubt, the office handling your case or a qualified
professional can confirm which path applies.
