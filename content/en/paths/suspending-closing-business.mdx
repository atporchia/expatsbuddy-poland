A JDG owner in CEIDG can generally **suspend** their business activity for
any reason — for example between contracts, lower-than-expected income, or
taking up employment — for a minimum of 30 days, up to and including
suspending indefinitely. Official sources describe suspension as free of
charge.

While a business is suspended, official sources describe the owner as
generally not performing business activity or earning revenue from it, and
not owing ZUS contributions or filing VAT declarations for that period —
though the exact effects depend on individual circumstances, which the
official pages and an accountant can confirm.

**Resuming** works differently depending on how you suspended:

- if you suspended for a **fixed period** with a chosen resumption date,
  official sources describe the business as resuming **automatically** on
  that date, with no extra application needed;
- if you suspended **indefinitely**, or want to change the resumption date,
  you generally need to notify CEIDG with a separate application.

**Closing** a business is a distinct, final step from suspension — it removes
the business from CEIDG rather than pausing it. Official sources describe
closing, like suspension and resumption, as handled through biznes.gov.pl.

Because the consequences for taxes, ZUS, and any relief periods you may be
using (such as ulga na start) can depend on your specific situation, the
official pages below and an accountant are the reference before you suspend,
resume, or close a business.
