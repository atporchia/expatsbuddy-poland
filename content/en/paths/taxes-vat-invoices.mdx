A JDG owner generally has two separate tax questions to work out: **which
income-tax form** applies to their business income, and **whether and when
VAT** applies to their sales.

Official biznes.gov.pl sources describe three forms of personal income tax
(PIT) available to most JDG owners:

- **skala podatkowa** (tax scale) — the default form if none other is
  chosen; progressive rates and broad access to tax reliefs;
- **podatek liniowy** (flat-rate tax) — a single rate regardless of income,
  but official sources note it can reduce access to certain reliefs;
- **ryczałt** (lump-sum tax on registered revenue) — tax calculated on
  revenue rather than profit, at rates that vary by activity type; not every
  activity qualifies.

Which form applies, and which one is worth choosing, depends on individual
circumstances — this page only names the options; the official pages and an
accountant are the reference for choosing.

On **VAT**: official sources describe most businesses as needing to register
once their sales exceed a revenue threshold that biznes.gov.pl publishes and
updates, though some businesses register voluntarily below that threshold —
for example to recover VAT on their own purchases, or because their clients
expect VAT invoices. The threshold itself changes periodically, so this page
does not state a figure; check the official VAT pages linked below for the
current one.

On **invoices** (*faktura*): official sources note that invoices for foreign
clients can be issued in a foreign language, but VAT accounting itself must
be kept in Polish złoty. VAT payers have specific content requirements for
what an invoice must include.
