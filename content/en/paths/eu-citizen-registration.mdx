If you hold citizenship of an **EU country, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein, or
Switzerland**, your stay in Poland works differently from the residence-permit
system for non-EU citizens. You do not apply for a temporary residence permit
or a *karta pobytu*. Instead, official sources describe a **registration of
residence** (*rejestracja pobytu*) for stays longer than 3 months.

The registration is handled by the **voivodeship office** for the place where
you live, and official sources say the application is submitted in person.
After registration you can be issued a **certificate of registration of
residence of an EU citizen** — a document that banks, employers, and other
institutions often ask about. Official sources also note that not registering
can result in a fine, and that registration itself is free.

On work: official sources state that EU/EFTA/Swiss citizens generally have
**free access to the Polish labour market** and do not need a work permit.
The registration duty is about documenting a longer stay, not about work
authorization.

Documents mentioned in official sources depend on your purpose of stay — for
example employment documents for workers or enrolment documents for students.
