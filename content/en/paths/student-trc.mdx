Official sources describe a temporary residence permit granted for the purpose
of **studies** (*zezwolenie na pobyt czasowy w celu kształcenia się na
studiach*). It is the residence basis most often mentioned for non-EU citizens
whose main reason for staying in Poland is a full-time study programme.

The general idea in official sources: the education has to justify a stay
longer than 3 months, and the applicant is asked to document things like
**admission or enrolment at the university**, **health insurance**, and
**financial means** for living costs. The permit is decided by the voivode of
the region where you live, and applications are prepared through the official
MOS portal.

Studying is one of several residence bases. Working, joining family, and other
circumstances each have their own official path, with different document
lists. If your situation mixes several elements — for example you study and
also work — the official MOS pages describe which basis fits which situation,
and the office deciding your case makes the final call.

If an application is missing formal elements, the office sends a request for
filling formal gaps (*braki formalne*) with a deadline it sets. Because
requirements and lists change, always check the current official MOS pages
linked below.
