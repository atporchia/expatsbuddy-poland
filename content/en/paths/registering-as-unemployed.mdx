Registering at the **poviat labour office** (*powiatowy urząd pracy*, often
just *urząd pracy*) is how a person without work enters the public employment
system. Registration gives you a formal status — **unemployed person** (*osoba
bezrobotna*) or **jobseeker** (*osoba poszukująca pracy*) — and access to
labour-market services such as job placement.

Two things are commonly confused:

- **Registering as unemployed** — the administrative status itself;
- **Unemployment benefit** (*zasiłek dla bezrobotnych*) — a payment with its
  own separate conditions. Registration does not automatically mean a benefit
  is paid; official sources describe the conditions separately.

For foreigners, official ministry pages explain that rights at the labour
office depend on **legal status** and specific conditions — and that after
registration, an unemployed foreigner may be covered by labour-market services
and **health insurance** on the same conditions as Polish citizens. Official
sources describe eligibility to register at all as varying by residence
basis — for example, EU/EFTA/Swiss citizens, holders of permanent or
long-term resident status, and holders of certain temporary residence permits
are each treated differently. Which group applies to you, and what conditions
come with it, is for the labour office to confirm — this page only tells you
that the distinction exists.

Official document lists for registration mention an identity document, a
residence title for foreigners (for example a decision or residence card),
employment certificates, and school certificates, diplomas, or qualification
documents. Having a foreign diploma does not, by itself, block registration.
Whether that diploma also needs to be formally **recognized** (nostrified) is
a separate question from registration, and depends on the profession: for
regulated professions (for example doctor, nurse, or architect), official
sources describe diploma recognition as generally required before practicing;
for most other jobs, there is no government recognition step, and an employer
decides directly whether to accept a foreign diploma. NAWA's KWALIFIKATOR
database can help you check how a specific foreign diploma is generally
regarded in Poland.

Registration itself is typically handled at the labour office for where you
live, and parts of it can be started online through praca.gov.pl.
