Health insurance for a self-employed person in Poland runs through the same
system as for employees, but the payment path is different. Official sources
describe it this way: registering a JDG through CEIDG registers you with
**ZUS** as a payer, and paying the monthly **health contribution**
(*składka zdrowotna*) to ZUS is what keeps you covered by the **National
Health Fund (NFZ)** — the same public healthcare system employees are
covered through, but funded by the entrepreneur directly instead of through
an employer.

Official biznes.gov.pl and ZUS sources describe the health contribution as
mandatory for entrepreneurs registered in CEIDG, with the exact amount
depending on the **taxation form** chosen for the business (for example tax
scale, flat-rate tax, or lump-sum tax) — different forms use different rules
to calculate the contribution base. Because these rules and amounts are
updated periodically, this page does not restate specific figures; the
official pages below carry the current ones.

If your health contribution is not paid, official sources describe
consequences for your NFZ coverage — another reason to treat the linked ZUS
and biznes.gov.pl pages as the reference for your specific situation, ideally
together with an accountant if you are unsure which taxation form applies to
you.
