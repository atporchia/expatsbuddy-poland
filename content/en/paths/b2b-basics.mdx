**B2B** (business-to-business) is not a specific legal term in Polish law —
it is the common way people describe an arrangement where a self-employed
person, usually running a **JDG**, provides services to a client company
under a **civil-law contract** (for example a contract for specific work or
a contract of mandate), instead of an **employment contract**.

The practical difference official sources point to: an employment contract is
governed by the Labour Code and gives the employee specific protections
(minimum wage, paid leave, notice periods, and so on). A B2B arrangement is
governed by the Civil Code instead, generally offers more flexibility for
both sides, and shifts responsibilities onto the self-employed person —
including registering a JDG, issuing **invoices** (*faktura*) to the client,
and paying their own taxes and ZUS contributions.

Under B2B, the self-employed person is not the client's employee. They
typically:

- register and maintain their own JDG in CEIDG;
- issue invoices to the client, usually monthly;
- handle their own tax filings and ZUS contributions, described in more
  detail on the taxes and ZUS pages linked below.

Official sources note that eligibility to register a JDG (and therefore to
work under B2B) depends on residence status for non-EU/EEA/Swiss/US
citizens. Whether a B2B arrangement suits a specific person's situation —
compared with employment — depends on individual circumstances that this
page does not evaluate.
