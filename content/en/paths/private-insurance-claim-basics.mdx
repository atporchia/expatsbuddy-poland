Private insurance in Poland — including the group insurance many employers
offer — is a **contract**. What it pays for, how much, and under which
conditions is defined by the policy and its general terms (*OWU — ogólne
warunki ubezpieczenia*). That is why no general page, including this one, can
say whether something is covered: the same event can be covered under one
policy and excluded under another.

The general shape of a claim (*roszczenie*) that insurers describe:

1. an insured event happens (for example a hospital stay or surgery);
2. the insured person notifies the insurer, usually on a **claim form**;
3. the insurer asks for **documentation** — medical documents such as the
   hospital discharge document are commonly mentioned;
4. the insurer issues a **decision**.

If you disagree with a decision, official sources describe a **complaint**
(*reklamacja*) route: complaints to financial institutions are regulated by
law, with defined response timelines, and the **Rzecznik Finansowy (Financial
Ombudsman)** can assist customers after the insurer's complaint procedure is
exhausted. The Ministry of Finance page below describes the broader
policyholder-protection framework, and the Ombudsman's pages describe how
complaints and interventions work.

For employer-provided group insurance, your employer's HR is usually the
practical first contact for policy documents and claim forms.
