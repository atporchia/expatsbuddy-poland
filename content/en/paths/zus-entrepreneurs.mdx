Registering a JDG through CEIDG automatically registers you with **ZUS** as
the person responsible for your own social insurance contributions —
unlike an employee, where the employer handles most of this. Official
sources describe entrepreneurs as generally paying for: pension insurance,
disability insurance, accident insurance, the Labour Fund, and the health
contribution (*składka zdrowotna*); sickness insurance is usually voluntary
for entrepreneurs.

New entrepreneurs are often eligible for a sequence of reliefs, described by
ZUS and biznes.gov.pl as generally following in this order:

1. **Ulga na start** — for a set period, no social insurance contributions
   are owed (only the health contribution), for people starting a JDG for
   the first time or after a long break;
2. **Preferential contributions** — a further period of reduced social
   insurance contributions after ulga na start ends;
3. **Mały ZUS Plus** — available afterward (or to other qualifying small
   entrepreneurs), contributions are calculated in proportion to income
   rather than a flat declared base, subject to revenue thresholds and time
   limits.

Whether a specific entrepreneur qualifies for each stage, and the exact
amounts due, are questions the official ZUS and biznes.gov.pl pages answer —
and they change over time, so this page intentionally does not quote figures.
eZUS/PUE is where entrepreneurs generally manage their ZUS account and
declarations electronically.
