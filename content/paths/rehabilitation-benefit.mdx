**Świadczenie rehabilitacyjne** (rehabilitation benefit) is described in
official sources as a benefit for people who have **used up their sickness
benefit period but remain unable to work**, where further treatment or
rehabilitation offers a prospect of recovering the ability to work. In other
words: it is the step official sources describe *after* sickness benefit, not
a parallel or alternative payment.

The documents official ZUS sources list for this situation include:

- **ZNp-7** — the application for rehabilitation benefit;
- **OL-9** — a health certificate about your condition, completed by the
  treating doctor;
- **OL-10** — an occupational interview from the workplace, mentioned for
  some situations (official pages describe the exceptions).

Two useful things to understand: first, timing is discussed in official
sources because the benefit connects to the end of the sickness-benefit
period; second, **ZUS decides entitlement**, and its assessment includes a
medical evaluation by a ZUS doctor (*lekarz orzecznik*), not only the
paperwork. Neither this page nor any checklist can predict that outcome.

The official pages below list the current documents, the situations they
apply to (including after insurance has ended), and how submissions work,
including through eZUS/PUE.
