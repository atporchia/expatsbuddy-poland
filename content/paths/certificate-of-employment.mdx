The **świadectwo pracy** (certificate of employment) is a document your
employer issues **when the employment relationship ends** — whether by
termination or expiry of the contract. Official sources describe it as a
summary of the completed employment: the period worked, the type of work, the
mode in which the employment ended, and other information used later by
institutions.

It matters after employment ends because other procedures commonly refer to
it. For example, official Public Employment Services pages list employment
certificates among the documents used when **registering as unemployed**, and
employment history documents can matter in **unemployment-benefit** contexts.
A future employer may also ask for it.

Two documents are easy to confuse:

- the **employment contract** (*umowa o pracę*) — signed when employment
  starts, describing the terms of the job;
- the **certificate of employment** (*świadectwo pracy*) — issued when
  employment ends, summarizing what happened.

Official sources also describe what an employee can do if the employer fails
to issue the certificate or issues an incorrect one — including requesting a
correction (*sprostowanie*) and, in disputes, the labour court route. The
details and time limits are described on the official pages below; for a
dispute with an employer, the labour office or a qualified professional can
explain your options.
