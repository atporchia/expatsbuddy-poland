While you are employed, your employer is involved in sick-leave payments. When
the employment ends, the sickness insurance connected to it ends too — but
official sources describe situations where **sickness benefit** (*zasiłek
chorobowy*) can still be relevant **after the insurance has ended**. In that
situation the institution involved is **ZUS**, not the former employer.

The official descriptions put a lot of weight on **dates and insurance
status**: when the incapacity for work began, how long it lasts, and when the
insurance ended. For example, gov.pl describes cases where the incapacity
arose within a defined period after the insurance ended and lasted a minimum
time. These time conditions are precise and change with the law, so the
official pages are the reference — and ZUS decides how they apply to a
specific person.

Forms commonly mentioned in this situation:

- **e-ZLA** — the electronic sick note a doctor issues; it goes to ZUS
  electronically;
- **ZAS-53** — an application for sickness benefit;
- **Z-10** — a statement used with claims for periods after insurance ended;
- **Z-3** — a certificate completed by the employer as the contribution payer.

Official ZUS sources describe which documents belong to which situation and
how they can be submitted, including electronically through eZUS/PUE. If you
are in this situation, the safest path is to read the official pages below and
confirm with ZUS what applies to you.
