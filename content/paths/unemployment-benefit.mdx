**Zasiłek dla bezrobotnych** (unemployment benefit) is a payment for people
registered as unemployed who meet conditions defined in law. It is a separate
thing from the registration itself: being registered gives you a status and
access to services, while the benefit has its **own conditions** that the
labour office checks.

Official sources describe the general shape of those conditions. For example,
they mention a work-history condition — often summarized as at least **365
days** of qualifying work (with at least minimum wage and required
contributions) within the **18 months** before registration — and explain that
entitlement is counted **from the registration date** when the other
conditions are met. They also describe how the benefit period and amount vary
with employment history. The exact rules, amounts, and exceptions are on the
official pages, and they change over time.

For foreigners there is an extra layer: whether labour-office rights apply
depends on **legal status**, as described on the official ministry pages. This
is exactly the kind of detail ExpatsBuddy does not decide — the labour office
assesses each registration and benefit case individually. Use the official
sources below to read the current conditions, and treat the office's decision
as the answer for your situation.
