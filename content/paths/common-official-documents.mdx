Most official documents a foreigner meets in Poland belong to one of a few
**families**, each owned by a different institution. Recognizing the family
tells you where to look for official information and which office to contact.

**ZUS forms** — social-insurance paperwork, with codes like **ZAS-53**
(sickness-benefit application), **Z-3** (payer certificate), **Z-10**
(statement for after-insurance periods), **ZNp-7** (rehabilitation-benefit
application), and **OL-9** (doctor's health certificate). Owner: **ZUS**; the
official form pages are on zus.pl.

**Residence documents** — connected to legal stay: the temporary residence
application, the **request for filling formal gaps** (*wezwanie do
uzupełnienia braków formalnych*), and the **administrative decision**
(*decyzja*) that ends a procedure. Owner: **MOS / Office for Foreigners** and
the voivodeship offices.

**Employer documents** — the **employment contract**, the **termination
notice** (*wypowiedzenie*), and the **świadectwo pracy** (certificate of
employment) issued when employment ends. Owner: your **employer**, with rules
described on gov.pl.

**Medical documents** — the **hospital discharge document** (*karta
informacyjna leczenia szpitalnego*), **e-referrals**, and SOR documentation,
increasingly available electronically through **IKP**. Owner: the **hospital
or clinic**, with the system described on pacjent.gov.pl.

**Insurance documents** — the **claim form**, the **insurer's decision**, and
the **complaint response** (*odpowiedź na reklamację*). Owner: your **private
insurer**, within the policyholder-protection framework described by the
Ministry of Finance and the Financial Ombudsman.

If you cannot place a document, its letterhead and the institution named in
the footer are usually the fastest clue — then the matching family above
points you to the official source.
